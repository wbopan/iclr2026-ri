Motivation
Making LLMs Factual is Hard

LLMs face an inherent tension: reducing hallucination increases over-refusal, and vice versa.

Measuring Knowledge-Aware Refusal is Even Harder

Different models land at different points on the accuracy-refusal plane. But which one makes better refusal decisions?

2
Method
Definition: Refusal Index
RI = ρₛ = Corr(Rank(rᵢ), Rank(wᵢ))
Spearman rank correlation between refusal probability rᵢ and error probability wᵢ
Perfect knowledge-awareness: wᵢ ≤ wⱼ ⇔ P(refuse xᵢ) ≤ P(refuse xⱼ)
Accuracy-Refusal Curve

Higher RI = more convex curve = better knowledge-aware refusal.

Estimation via Gaussian Copula
1
Observe refusal Rᵢ from Pass 1 and correctness Ŵᵢ from Pass 2
2
Compute thresholds: τᴿ = Φ⁻¹(1−r), τᵂ = Φ⁻¹(1−μ)
3
Maximize multinomial log-likelihood ℓ(ρ) to estimate ρ̂
4
Convert to Spearman: ρₛ = (6/π) arcsin(ρ̂/2)

Key Take-Aways
1. RI identifies persistent capability gaps in factual tasks that accuracy alone overlooks.
2. Training pipelines and model family are the strongest predictors of knowledge-aware refusal, not model scale.
3. Knowledge-aware refusal degrades when ground truth is unavailable in context, suggesting over-reliance on contextual cues.
